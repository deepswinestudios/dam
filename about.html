Our Story

The Dance Attack Family

Est. 1995
John & Cookie

"Every dancer who walks through our doors becomes part of our family. That has always been — and will always be — what Dance Attack is about."

Dance Attack Miami was founded in 1995 by John Culbertson and Cookie Ramos with a single belief: that dance transforms lives. For over 30 years, they have built not just a studio, but a community — a family where every dancer, from their very first plié to their professional debut, is cared for, challenged, and celebrated.

Their alumni have performed on Broadway, toured with Disney, appeared on Glee and Dancing with the Stars, and shared stages with Beyoncé, Jennifer Lopez, and more. But that was never the point. The point has always been the family.

— John & Cookie, Founders

What to Wear & How to Act
Attire & Etiquette

Hair & Attire

  • Ballet — Hair in a bun, studio leotard, tights, ballet slippers. Males: white T-shirt, black shorts, ballet slippers.
  • Jazz, Lyrical, Acro, Tap — Studio shorts & top, hair pulled away from face. Proper shoes for each class.
  • Ballroom — Studio skirt required. Ballroom heels.
  • Hip-Hop — Baggy clothing and sneakers. Hats and accessories welcome.
  • John & Cookie Classes — Studio Lycra shorts. Female: studio half top. Males: tank top or T-shirt.

Contact the Studio Manager about ordering studio attire.

Studio Etiquette

  • Arrive early or on time. Punctuality is paramount. Stretch independently.
  • Place belongings in the dressing area, away from the dance floor.
  • Adhere to the dress code in footwear, clothing, and hair. Remove all jewelry.
  • Silence your mobile phone. No texting or calls during class time.
  • Be quiet during class. Do not correct other students.
  • Respect your instructors and fellow dancers at all times.
  • Absolutely no food or drinks allowed in the studios.
  • Students may not leave without parental/guardian permission.
